\question (10 points) 
The power series for $\sin x$ is $\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}x^{2n+1}$. Evaluate the definite integral as a power series
$$
\int_{0}^2 \frac{\sin x}{x}\ dx
$$

Your solution should be a series, but you need not evaluate the series.
